STILL OPEN! AMCS 4850: Global Synod


The course examines historical, theological, and practical themes tied to the Catholic Church’s Synod on Synodality. It will also explore Ignatian history and spirituality as a lens to consider the Synod’s implications for members of Jesuit institutions in a global context. Participants in the course will travel to Rome during the Synod on Synodality.

CACS Events

Communitas Dinner: Faith & Flowers-- A Conversation with Professor Jim Robinson

On April 19th, our cultural liaisons invited Prof. Robinson (Iona University) for a dinner conversation in which we explored the idea of finding God in all things, finding our place in the web of life, and finding our way to actively embody sustainability and justice.

Screening and Conversation with Ethan Hawke on Wildcat

On May 3rd, we co-sponsored a screening of Wildcat followed by a panel discussion with director Ethan Hawke, Fordham’s resident Flannery O’Connor expert, Angela Alaimo O’Donnell, and Fordham CRC director David Gibson.

DION: The Bronx’s “Wanderer” Returns

On April 22nd, we hosted a conversation & performance featuring music legend & Belmont native son Dion DiMucci. For those who could not make it, as well as for those members of the sold-out audience who would like to listen again, the event recording is now available on our YouTube channel.
