2023 Welcome Back and Open House Reception

On September 15th, we hosted our annual Welcome Back and Open House Reception. We have over 40 new impressive applicants to the program. We will be sending more information by the end of October to welcome our new class.

         Photo Credit: The Manitoban/Youtube

Communitas Outing: Irish Playwright Brian Friel's Translations Nov. 3 (Irish Repertory Theatre)

Translations is an Irish play set in Baile Beag (Ballybeg), a County Donegal village in 19th century Ireland. The play deals with a wide range of issues, stretching from love to language and communication, to Irish history, and to cultural imperialism.

Alumni Success

CACS alumni, Joseph Marutollo (FCRH ‘07), was recently appointed to serve as a U.S. Magistrate Judge in the Eastern District of New York (Brooklyn). He was sworn in on September 25th, 2023. In his biography, the Court included a reference to his work as a member of the Board of Advisors at The Curran Center.

Congratulations, Joe!

CACS Events

Dante Behind Bars: “Not Made to Live Like Brutes”

On September 19th, Ron Jenkins gave a lecture Drawing on his experience facilitating Dante workshops in prisons in Italy, Indonesia, and the United States.

Cardinal Cupich on the Enduring Legacy

of Cardinal Joseph Bernardin

On September 26th, we had the honor of co-hosting a lecture by Chicago Cardinal Blase Cupich. Cardinal Cupich delivered a powerful update to the landmark “consistent ethic of life” talk that his predecessor, the late Cardinal Joseph Bernardin, delivered 40 years earlier at Fordham.

The 19th Annual Rita Cassella Jones Lecture

on Women and U.S. Catholicism

On October 3rd, Dr. Nancy Pineda-Madrid explored Christian thinking on salvation in light of the horrible violence against women across the Americas.

The 272: The Families Who Were Enslaved and Sold to Build the American Catholic Church

On October 12th, author Rachel L. Swarns discussed her new book, The 272, which follows one family through nearly two centuries of indentured servitude and enslavement to illuminate the harrowing origin story of Georgetown University and the Catholic Church in the United States.  

Upcoming Events

The Enduring Legacy of the UCA Martyrs

The Curran Center Commemorates the 34th anniversary of the UCA Martyrs in a 2 Part Event

Part 1: Screening of Llegaron de Noche

Wednesday, November 1st, 2023

3:00 pm | Flom Auditorium |Rose Hill
No Registration Required.


On November 16, 1989 six Jesuit priests, their cook, and her daughter were murdered at the UCA, the Jesuit University of El Salvador. This new film tells the story of that horrible night and

of Lucia Cerna, its only witness. Her brave testimony identified the assassins as soldiers of the Salvadoran military.

Part 2: The Enduring Legacy of the UCA Martyrs

Thursday, November 2nd, 2023

5:30pm | Tognino Hall | Rose Hill


Fr. José María Tojeira, SJ was the Jesuit Provincial at the time of the UCA martyrs. In this lecture, he will not only share stories about these remarkable figures, but will reflect on how their legacy continues to challenge us today.
